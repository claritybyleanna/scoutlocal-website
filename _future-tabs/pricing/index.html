02 / why Promote

Beacon turns “open” into “discovered.”

Most local businesses don't lose customers because nobody likes them. They lose them because nobody knew they were open, in stock, or around the corner — at the one moment it mattered.

Beacon is that moment. Tap go-live and every family who follows you sees you're open right now, where you are, and what's waiting. No ad spend. No algorithm. Just the people who already chose you, nudged at exactly the right time.

Turn dead hours into a line

Slow Tuesday at 2pm? Light a Beacon with a one-hour deal and watch the door. You control the timing, so you create demand when you need it — not when an ad calendar says so.

Sell the fresh batch before it cools

New tray out of the oven, last ten slots of the day, a surprise restock — announce it the second it's true. Urgency you can't fake is urgency that sells.

Reach a warm audience, free

Beacon pushes only to families who tapped “Notify me.” They asked to hear from you. That's a following most businesses pay thousands to build — and yours starts the day you upgrade.

Be found where you are, not where you were

For anyone who moves, Beacon drops a live pin so today's spot is never a guess. The family two blocks away finally knows you're there.

03 / built for how you work

Especially good if you don't have a storefront.

Home-based & by-appointment

Your Beacon is your sign.

No window on the highway, no walk-by traffic — and you don't need it. When you open booking, start a porch-pickup window, or drop a fresh batch, the nearby families who follow you get the nudge. One tap reaches everyone who's waiting on you, instead of posting to five feeds and hoping an algorithm shows it. The bakers, lash artists, detailers, and tutors run their whole day on it.

Food trucks & mobile vendors

Your location is your business. It changes daily.

A static listing can't keep up with a truck that parks somewhere new every shift. Beacon's live pin moves with you: go live the minute you set up, and every follower sees exactly where you are right now. When the rush fades, drop a flash deal to refill the line before close. Markets, lots, driveways, pop-ups — wherever today's spot is, your regulars can find it.

04 / a day on Promote

How a Friday could go.

A picture of Promote in action

Camp Street Tacos is a food truck and it just opened right outside of the main gate. On Free, it had a listing: photos, menu, a map pin. Nice to have, but families only found it if they happened to drive by the food truck.

On Promote, Friday looks different. The truck parks by the commissary at 11:00 and lights a Beacon: “Open now! Corner of Mahan & 3rd.” The forty-some families who'd tapped “Notify me” over the past month get a single push. By 11:20 there's a line of people who knew that lunch was two minutes away.

At 1:30 the rush thins and there's a tray of carnitas that won't keep. One more Beacon: “Last batch! $2 off till 2.” The deal clears the tray and the afternoon doesn't end on slow. That night the owner checks analytics: directions tapped, new followers, which Beacon pulled hardest.

Same truck, same tacos. The difference is that the people already rooting for it finally knew where it was and when.
